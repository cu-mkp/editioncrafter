{left margin} Juan Manuel In this parish of Taos on the seventh day of the month of September of one thousand eight hundred and thirty-nine: I, parish priest Don Antonio José Martínez, solemnly baptized and placed holy oils and chrism on an Indian child, seven or eight years old, who was rescued from the Ute tribe. Although it has been one year since he was rescued, he is still not sufficiently instructed, but being ill, and with the little that he has learned, he has effectively explained that he desires to join the religion of Jesus Christ. I verified that he is a servant of Don Charles Beaubien, resident of San Fernández. His godparents were Eugenio [Letendosen?] and María Rufina Vigil, also from San Fernández. I advised them of their obligation and spiritual kinship. I signed it for the record. [^The name of the baptized child was Juan Manuel] Antonio José Martínez {rubric}
