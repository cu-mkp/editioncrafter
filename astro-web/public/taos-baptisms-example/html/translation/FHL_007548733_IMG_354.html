{left margin} Juan Lorenzo In this parish of Taos on the fourth day of the month of October of one thousand eight hundred and forty-four: I, parish priest Don Antonio José Martínez, solemnly baptized and placed holy oils and sacred chrism on a six-year-old child of the Ute tribe from which he was rescued. I named him Juan Lorenzo. He is a servant of Juan Ignacio Martínez, both residents of the town of San Francisco del Rancho. The godparents were Benito Martínez and María Rosalía Mascareñas, residents of the same place. I advised them of their obligation and spiritual kinship, and I signed it for the record. Antonio José Martínez {rubric} {left margin} María Soledad In this parish of Taos on the fourth day of the month of October of one thousand eight hundred and forty-four: I, parish priest Don Antonio José Martínez, solemnly baptized and placed holy oils and sacred chrism on a child, five years old. I named her María Soledad. She is a descendant of the Ute tribe, servant of Juan Ignacio Martínez, resident of San Francisco del Rancho. The godparents were José Rafael Martínez and María Rosa Martínez, residents of the same place. I advised them of their obligation and spiritual kinship, and I signed it for the record. There was no sign that he had been baptized. [He was] wrapped and completely bundled in a piece of an old sarape.
