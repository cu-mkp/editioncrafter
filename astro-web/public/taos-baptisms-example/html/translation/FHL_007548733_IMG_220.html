{left margin} María Catarina In this parish of Taos on the twenty-fourth day of the month of May of one thousand eight hundred and forty-two: I, parish priest Don Antonio José Martínez, solemnly baptized and placed holy oils and sacred chrism on a child, six years old, a descendant of the Ute tribe from which the citizen José Mondragón, resident of the town of San Francisco del Rancho, rescued her. Her godparents were Felipe Nerio Maes and María del Refugio Pacheco, residents of the same place. I advised them of their obligation and spiritual kinship, and I signed it for the record. Antonio José Martínez {rubric} {left margin} María de la Luz In this parish of Taos on the twenty-sixth day of the month of June of one thousand eight hundred and forty-two: I, priest Don José de la Cruz Vigil, as curate, solemnly baptized and placed holy oils and sacred chrism on a four-year-old child, and I named her María de la Luz. She is a descendant of the Ute tribe, servant of Don Juan Bolem, who rescued her, resident of San Francisco del Rancho. The godparents were Juan Bautista Bolem and María Luisa Archuleta, residents of the same place. I advised them of their obligation and spiritual kinship, and I signed it for the record. Antonio José Martínez {rubric} José Vigil {rubric} {left margin} José Santiago In this parish of Taos, on the twenty-sixth of the month of June of one thousand eight hundred and forty-two: Having been catechized according to what is prescribed for the baptism of adults, and having obtained competent instruction, I, parish priest Don Antonio José Martínez, according to the special rituals, solemnly exorcized, baptized, placed holy oils and sacred chrism on an adult, about thirteen years of age, a descendant of the Ute tribe. He is a servant of Don Juan Roles, who rescued him a few years ago. He has been educated in his house in the neighborhood of San Francisco del Rancho. I found him to be sufficiently instructed in the principles, means, and end of the Christian religion (and very enthusiastic, desirous, and aspiring to be received in her, with the faith and hope of obtaining eternal life). I named him José Santiago. His godparents were Manuel Antonio Sandoval and Margarita Martínez, all residents of San Francisco del Rancho. I advised them of their obligation and spiritual kinship, and I signed it for the record. Antonio José Martínez {rubric}
