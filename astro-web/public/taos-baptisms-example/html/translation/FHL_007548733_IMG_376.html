{left margin} María Luisa In this parish of Taos on the second day of the month of January of the year one thousand eight hundred and forty-four: I, parish priest Don Antonio José Martínez solemnly baptized, placed holy oils and sacred chrism on an adult, about thirty years old, according to the rite of our Holy Mother Church for the baptism of adults. I named her María Luisa. She is an Indian rescued from the Ute tribe whose baptism was preceded by the corresponding catechisms until she was well instructed in the principles, means, and ends of our holy Christian religion. She embraced it affectionately and fervently, and she abjured and renounced any errors of the barbarian gentiles in which she had been instructed. The said Indian is a servant of Don Charles Beaubien and Señora María Paula Lobato, residents of San Fernando. The godparents were Feliz [Nontin] and Luz Trujillo, residents of the same place.
